The *Taylor Expansion* of a function ($f(x)$) is an infinite sum of terms that represent the function about a point ($a$). 
$$
\begin{aligned}
f(x) &= f(a) + f'(a)(x - a) + \frac{f''(a)}{2!}(x - a)^{2} + \frac{f^{(3)}(a)}{3!}(x - a)^{3} + \cdots \\\\
&+ \frac{f^{(n)}(a)}{n!}(x - a)^{n} + \cdots
\end{aligned}
$$
